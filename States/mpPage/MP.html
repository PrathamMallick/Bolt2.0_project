Bagh Print

Bagh print is a traditional Indian handicraft that involves hand printing wood blocks using natural colors and dyes. The craft originated in Bagh, a village in the Dhar district of Madhya Pradesh, and is named after the village.

The print is characterized by a variety of geometric and floral designs made using vegetable dyes and finely cut wooden blocks.

Bamboo Craft

Bamboo craft in Madhya Pradesh is a reflection of the state's tribal lifestyle, and is used to make both functional and ornamental products. The state's major bamboo producing regions are Balaghat, Shahdol, Seoni, and Mandla.

The Baiga, Gond, and Korku tribal communities are known for their bamboo work, which combines traditional expertise with modern designs.
